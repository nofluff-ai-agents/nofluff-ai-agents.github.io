Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 — UAE Labour Law End of Service Gratuity (EOSB) is a mandatory payment owed to employees upon termination of employment. It is calculated based on basic salary only — allowances are excluded.
The EOSB formula — step by step
1

Calculate the daily basic salary rate

Divide the employee's monthly basic salary by 30 days. This is your daily rate — the foundation of every EOSB calculation. Allowances (housing, transport, phone) are NOT included.

Daily rate = Basic salary ÷ 30
2

Calculate total years of service

Count the full years and partial months from the official start date to the last working day. Partial years are prorated — a 6-month partial year counts as 0.5 years.

Tenure = End date − Start date (in years)
3

Apply the correct gratuity rate per year

The rate depends on total tenure. The first 5 years are calculated at 21 days per year. Any service beyond 5 years is calculated at 30 days per year. These are calculated separately then added together.

Years 1–5: 21 days/year · Years 5+: 30 days/year
4

Multiply days by daily rate

Multiply the total gratuity days earned by the daily basic salary rate to get the gross EOSB amount before any deductions.

EOSB = Total gratuity days × Daily rate
5

Apply resignation reduction (if applicable)

If the employee resigned — rather than being terminated — a reduction may apply depending on tenure. Less than 1 year: no gratuity. 1–3 years: one third. 3–5 years: two thirds. Over 5 years: full gratuity regardless of resignation or termination.

Termination = Full · Resignation varies by tenure

Edge cases to watch for
⚠

Unpaid leave deductions

Days of unpaid leave must be subtracted from total service before calculating EOSB. Keep accurate records of all unpaid absence throughout employment.

i

Salary changes during tenure

EOSB is always calculated on the final basic salary at the time of termination — not an average. A recent salary increase benefits the employee significantly.

⚠

Limited vs unlimited contracts

Under the 2021 law, all new contracts are effectively unlimited. However legacy limited contracts may carry different termination implications — always check the original contract type.

!

Termination for cause

Under Article 44 of Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021, an employer may terminate without EOSB in cases of gross misconduct. This requires proper documentation and due process — seek legal advice before withholding gratuity.

i

Freezone employees

Some freezones (DIFC, ADGM) have their own employment regulations which may differ from mainland UAE Labour Law. Always verify the applicable law for your entity type.
