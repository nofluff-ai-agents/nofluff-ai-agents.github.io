Step 1 — Copy this prompt to get your written analysis
Please fill in the details below before using this prompt. Delete any lines that are not applicable to your situation. --- ABOUT MY ORGANISATION: - Company name: [your company name] - Function or team being analysed: [e.g. HR team / Sales team / All departments] - Total number of employees in the survey: [e.g. 24 employees] - Rating scale used: [e.g. 1–5 where 1 = no proficiency and 5 = expert] - Does the data include manager ratings alongside self-ratings? [Yes / No] - Required proficiency level for the role (if known): [e.g. All skills should be at 3.5 or above — delete if unknown] SKILLS ASSESSED IN THE SURVEY: [List the exact skill column names from your CSV so Claude can reference them correctly] - [Skill 1 name] - [Skill 2 name] - [Skill 3 name] - [Add more as needed] --- I have uploaded a CSV file containing L&D survey responses from my team. Please analyse the data and provide a complete Learning Needs Analysis report including: 1. SKILL GAP SUMMARY - Average score per skill across the whole team - Rank skills from most critical gap to strongest - Flag which gaps are organisation-wide (everyone needs it) vs targeted (only some departments or levels) 2. HEATMAP BREAKDOWN - Average score per skill broken down by department and seniority level - Highlight the most critical combinations (e.g. junior HRBP team on data analytics) 3. BLIND SPOT DETECTION (if manager ratings are included) - Identify skills where self-rating significantly exceeds manager rating - Flag these as blind spots — employees who are overconfident will not respond to standard training - Recommend awareness-first interventions for these skills 4. AUDIENCE SEGMENTS - Group employees into 2–3 learning profiles based on their gap patterns - Name each segment and describe what they need and why - Suggest a different learning approach for each segment 5. PRIORITISED INTERVENTION PLAN - For each skill gap, recommend the most appropriate intervention type: workshop, e-learning, coaching, mentoring, or on-the-job - Explain why that intervention type suits this specific gap - Suggest a timeline and flag whether this is individual coaching vs group training - Note estimated relative effort: high / medium / low investment 6. BUDGET GUIDANCE - Which gaps offer the highest ROI if addressed centrally (organisation-wide training) - Which gaps are better addressed individually (targeted coaching rather than group training) Present as a structured report with clear sections. Use plain language — this report will be shared with senior leadership who are not L&D specialists.
