Strategic Influence
Ability to shape business decisions through people strategy
+
Main question
"Tell me about a time you influenced a senior business leader to change their approach to a people-related decision. What was the situation and how did you bring them around?"
What to look for: Evidence of data-driven persuasion, comfort challenging authority, outcome focus, and ability to build trust with senior stakeholders.
Follow-up probe
"What would have happened if you hadn't intervened? How did you measure the impact of your influence?"
What to look for: Commercial awareness, accountability for outcomes, ability to quantify people impact.
Scoring guide
4
Clear example, measurable outcome, influenced at C-suite level, data-backed approach
3
Good example, positive outcome, influenced at VP level, some data used
2
Vague example, unclear outcome, limited stakeholder seniority
1
No clear example, theoretical answer, no evidence of influence
2
Organisational Design
Experience shaping team structures to support business growth
+
Main question
"Describe a situation where you led or contributed to a significant organisational redesign. What triggered it, how did you approach it, and what was the result?"
What to look for: Structured methodology, stakeholder management, change management skills, commercial rationale, and ability to manage ambiguity.
Follow-up probe
"How did you handle the people who were negatively affected by the redesign? What would you do differently next time?"
What to look for: Empathy, fairness, legal awareness, and genuine reflection on lessons learned.
Scoring guide
4
Led full redesign, clear methodology, measured business impact, handled people sensitively
3
Contributed significantly, good process, positive outcome, some people challenges managed
2
Limited involvement, unclear process, outcome not well articulated
1
No direct experience, theoretical answer only
3
Data-Driven Decision Making
Using people analytics to drive informed recommendations
+
Main question
"Give me an example of a time you used people data to identify a problem that wasn't yet visible to leadership. What did you find and what action did it lead to?"
What to look for: Proactive use of data, ability to translate numbers into narrative, courage to surface uncomfortable findings, and commercial impact.
Follow-up probe
"What tools or methods did you use? How did you present the findings to make them compelling to a non-HR audience?"
What to look for: Technical competence with data tools, storytelling ability, audience awareness.
Scoring guide
4
Proactively identified trend, presented compelling narrative, drove measurable business change
3
Used data reactively but effectively, good presentation, positive outcome
2
Basic data use, limited storytelling, outcome unclear
1
Minimal data experience, gut-feel approach only
4
Coaching & Leadership Development
Developing leaders and building management capability
+
Main question
"Tell me about a leader you coached who was struggling. What was the situation, how did you approach the coaching, and what changed as a result?"
What to look for: Coaching methodology, ability to build trust quickly, patience, measurable behaviour change, and appropriate escalation when needed.
Follow-up probe
"Was there ever a case where coaching wasn't enough and you had to take a different approach? How did you handle that?"
What to look for: Judgement, commercial pragmatism, courage to have difficult conversations, and HR process knowledge.
Scoring guide
4
Sustained behaviour change, clear methodology, trusted advisor relationship built
3
Positive coaching outcome, good approach, some evidence of methodology
2
Limited coaching experience, unclear outcome, more telling than coaching
1
No coaching experience, theoretical answer only
