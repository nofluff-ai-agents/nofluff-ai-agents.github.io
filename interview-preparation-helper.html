Organisational Design — strong match
Strong
Your CV clearly demonstrates org design experience — you led a restructure of the Operations function in 2023 affecting 45 people, with a documented 18% improvement in span of control. This directly addresses one of the JD's key competencies and should be prominent in your first interview response.
Interview tip Lead with this example when asked about strategic impact. Frame it as: situation → your approach → measurable outcome. Don't wait for them to ask — volunteer it early as proof of business partnering at scale.
Likely interview questions with your talking points 2 of 8 shown
Q1
"Tell me about a time you used people data to influence a senior business decision. What did you find and what happened as a result?"
Your talking point — drawn from your CV
Reference your Q3 2024 attrition analysis — you identified that 68% of voluntary leavers in Engineering had been in role for less than 18 months and had not received a development conversation in their first year. You presented this to the VP Engineering with a proposed onboarding enhancement. The initiative was approved and attrition in the cohort dropped by 22% over the following two quarters.
Acknowledge the data gap on your CV upfront — "My CV understates this, so let me give you the context directly." Interviewers respect candidates who self-correct confidently.
Q2
"This role requires working closely with Engineering and Product leadership. How do you build credibility quickly with technical stakeholders who may be sceptical of HR?"
Your talking point — drawn from your CV
Your CV shows 2 years embedded with the Technology function at your previous employer. Draw on your approach there — you spent the first 30 days in listening mode, attending sprint retrospectives and engineering all-hands before making any recommendations. Your first proposal was presented in their language — headcount ROI, velocity impact — not HR language. This approach led to being described as a "business partner who actually understands how we work" in your last 360 feedback.
End with a question back: "Can you tell me what the Engineering VP's biggest people challenge is right now?" — shows strategic curiosity and signals you're already thinking like a business partner.
