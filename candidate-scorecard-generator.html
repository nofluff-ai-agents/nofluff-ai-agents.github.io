Two steps. First, generate your custom Google Sheets scorecard. Then optionally upload completed scores for an instant candidate comparison report.

1
Generate your Google Sheets template
Fill in your role and competency details. Claude generates a complete Google Sheets scorecard with formulas, weighted scoring, auto-summary per candidate, and a comparison tab — ready for your entire panel to use simultaneously.
Step 1 prompt — copy and fill in your details
Please fill in the details below before using this prompt. Delete any lines that are not applicable to your situation. --- ROLE DETAILS: - Job title: [e.g. Senior HR Business Partner] - Seniority level: [e.g. Senior, Manager, Director] - Company name: [your company name] - Number of candidates to score: [e.g. 5 candidates] - Number of interviewers on the panel: [e.g. 3 interviewers] - Interview stage: [e.g. Final round, Panel interview] COMPETENCIES TO ASSESS: Choose one option and delete the other: Option A — I'll define my own: - Competency 1: [name] — Weight: [e.g. 30%] - Competency 2: [name] — Weight: [e.g. 25%] - Competency 3: [name] — Weight: [e.g. 25%] - Competency 4: [name] — Weight: [e.g. 20%] (Weights must total 100%) Option B — Extract from my JD: [Paste your full job description here] SCORING PREFERENCE: - Rating scale: [e.g. 1-4 or 1-5] - Any must-pass competencies (auto no-hire if scored 1): [list or leave blank] --- Please generate a complete Google Sheets candidate scorecard template with: 1. One tab per candidate (duplicate as needed for each new hire) 2. Each tab includes: candidate name, interviewer, date fields, competency rows with weights, score column (1-4 dropdown), weighted score formula, evidence notes column, follow-up flag column 3. Auto-generated summary section at the bottom of each tab showing: total weighted score, percentile, hire/hold/no-hire verdict, and a one-paragraph candidate summary 4. A Summary tab that pulls all candidate scores into a ranked comparison table with verdicts side by side 5. All Google Sheets formulas written out clearly with exact cell references so I can set it up immediately Format as clear step-by-step Google Sheets setup instructions.
Copied! Open Claude, paste it in, fill your details and hit send.
2
Upload scores for candidate comparison Optional
Once your panel has finished scoring, export the Google Sheet as CSV (File → Download → CSV). Upload it to Claude with the prompt below for an instant ranked comparison of all candidates.
Step 2 prompt — use after uploading your completed CSV
I have uploaded a CSV file with candidate interview scores from our hiring panel. Please analyse and provide: 1. A ranked candidate comparison table — sorted by total weighted score, highest to lowest 2. A competency breakdown showing how each candidate scored on each area — highlight standout strengths and gaps 3. Any scoring inconsistencies between interviewers for the same candidate — flag significant disagreements 4. A final shortlist recommendation with clear reasoning — hire, hold, or no hire per candidate 5. Any patterns worth noting — e.g. a candidate who scored low on one critical competency despite a high overall score Present as a clean structured report the hiring panel can review together in a debrief meeting.
Copied! Upload your CSV to Claude and paste this prompt alongside it.
